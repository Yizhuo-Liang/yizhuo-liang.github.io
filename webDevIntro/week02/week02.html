Week 2 Reading Reflections

As We May Think by Vannevar Bush

It feels amazing to read someone's imagination of the future from more than a century ago. Actually most of the predictions of the technology already took place in reality. Even the most convolutedly sophisticated logical and emotional thinking of human can be conducted by artifitial intelligence, which is impossible for the ones who live in 19th century to imagine. Sadly, the first problem indicated in the article is not resolved yet: people didn't really augment our own minds or our ways to understand and store informations. As the author says, it took a huge amount of time for scientists just to know what others found out from the academic papers. Now, collaboration between scientists is still base on academic papers. While the complexity of siences boomed when it gets more and more advanced and it's impossibble for a single man to study something, scientists still waste lots of time on understanding others papers. More generally, unlike the imagination of the author, human didn't create a real "second brain" in the last 150 years. The softwres who claim themselves "second brain" are still note taking softwares.


Long Live The Web by Tim Berners-Lee

This article, which was wrote 11 years ago, successfully predicted the issues of the internet. Sadly, the warnings of Mr.Berners-Lee didn't work out, where nowadays the web world still have problems of information freedom, privacy violation, government supervision abuse, etc... But, I believe people are still working on fixing those problems. Probably we have to wait longer, wait for web3 which is decentrialized rather than centralized web2.
