Phase 4 - Seeding

SEEDING. DISTRIBUTION.

The fourth phase of Garlic=Rich Air was like an epilogue. Leftover bulbs of garlic were taken back to the farm and replanted, seeding about 20,000 garlic plants to be harvested the next year. The site continued to be active for a while, as people prepared for another season of trading with the next harvest.

At the same time, more bulbs of garlic were taken to the Seattle National Alliance for Media, Arts, and Culture (NAMAC) conference. As they presented their project, the members of Garlic=Rich Air distributed more bulbs of garlic to the educators, critics, and artists that were all attending the conference. They hoped that people would take their bulbs back to their homes all over the USA and reseed their garlic across the country.

Beyond Phase 4, Garlic=Rich Air was left alone for as long as it survived, though it was eventually brought back by The New Museum and Melanie Hoff with the help of Cheang herself. The system was accessable only through the museum's local network, true to the original spirit of the project.
